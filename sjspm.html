§ 1 Framework Overview

What SJSPM™ Proposes

The dominant job search literature treats the search process as a skills-and-strategy problem: build a better résumé, network more effectively, interview more precisely. SJSPM™ does not reject this — it subordinates it to a more fundamental question: under what energetic conditions is the searcher operating when they execute those strategies?

SJSPM™ proposes that job search failure, underperformance, and settlement for suboptimal offers are frequently energy-mediated phenomena — not strategy failures. A depleted searcher executing a good strategy will produce inferior outcomes to an energy-intact searcher executing the same strategy. The model makes this mechanism explicit, measurable, and addressable.

The Sustainable Job Search Performance Model™ operationalizes Career Energy Management™ theory within the specific high-demand context of occupational transition. Job search imposes simultaneous and often unrelenting demands across all five CEM™ energy domains — making it one of the highest-risk environments for cumulative energy depletion in a working adult's career lifecycle. SJSPM™ provides the practitioner and researcher with a structured, domain-specific framework for assessing, protecting, and deploying job search energy toward sustainable, high-quality employment outcomes.
